Gabe
Clarke

Lauded as a true 'tenore di grazia' with a bright timbre and flawless technical command, Dutch-American tenor Gabe Clarke is quickly gaining recognition on the European stage. From the intricate fioritura of Rossini and the soaring high-tessitura roles of Bellini to the refined, lyrical poise of his Mozart, critics have celebrated his 'starry promise,' noting a rare combination of refined vocal elegance and a commanding, natural stage presence.

"Both tenors were impressive in their way, with Gabe Clarke as a finely lyrical Cavalier Belfiore for whom Rossinian fioriture held no terrors."

— R. Hugill

"The famous big ensembles come off under Hartinger, and there's starry promise from Jane Burnell (Contessa di Folleville), Ihor Mostovoi (Don Profondo) and Gabe Clarke (Cavalier Belfiore)."

— The Arts Desk

"Corinna has another admirer, the vain Chevalier Belfiore… sung by Dutch-American tenor Gabe Clarke… his aria 'Nel suo divin sembiante' was brilliantly sung."

— O. El Naggar
